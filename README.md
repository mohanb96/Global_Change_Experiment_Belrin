# Multiple GCFs Berlin Field Experiment

This repository contains data-processing and analysis code for the manuscript:

**Multiple global change factors driving directional change in plant communities**

The study investigates how increasing numbers of co-occurring global change factors (GCFs) affect grassland plant communities. We combine a multi-year field experiment in Berlin with a global grassland observational analysis to test whether factor number captures an additional dimension of ecosystem response under multidimensional environmental change.

## Study overview

Global change factors such as warming, drought, nutrient enrichment, pollution, salinity, microplastics, biocides and surfactants rarely occur in isolation. Although many single-factor effects are well studied, much less is known about how plant communities respond when multiple mechanistically distinct factors act simultaneously.

In the Berlin field experiment, we applied up to ten individual and co-occurring GCFs and monitored plant community responses over five years. The experimental design included:

- untreated control plots,
- ten single-factor treatments,
- randomly drawn multi-factor combinations containing 2, 4, 6, 8 or 10 GCFs,
- repeated vegetation surveys from the pre-treatment baseline through four post-treatment years.

The main plant community responses analysed were:

- plant species richness,
- plant total biomass,
- Shannon diversity,
- plant evenness,
- plant community composition.

The project also includes a global grassland observational analysis assessing whether increasing numbers of GCFs exceeding low to intermediate intensity thresholds are associated with reduced plant productivity and vegetation cover worldwide.
